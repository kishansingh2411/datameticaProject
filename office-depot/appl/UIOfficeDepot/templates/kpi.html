Order Conversion Rate (OCR)

Describes the likelihood that any individual visit the site will make a purchase.

Total Number of Orders Taken / Total Visits = OCR

Possible Impacting Factors

  • Poor Qualification – The number of visits to the site has gone up but the new traffic is poorly qualified
  • Visitor Confusion – The number of visits to the site has gone up but the visiting people can not find what they’re looking for
  • Real changes – The number of orders have actually dramatically increased or decreased relative to the number of visits to the site
Average Revenue per Order

Sum of Revenue Generated / Orders =Average Revenue per Order

Expectation:

The strategy for improving this performance indicator is to attract more valuable visitors to your website. Consider this KPI to critically examine each new visitor acquisition effort, segmentation as necessary, to determine whether different strategies are actually working

Possible Impacting Factors

  • Marketing campaigns
  • Operations inefficiencies
  • Issue with revenue realization path
Average Page Views per Visit

Excellent indicator of how compelling and easily navigated your visitors find your website

Page Views / Visits = Average Page Views per Visit

Expectation:

To increase this average indicating a greater interest on the part of the visitor. However, depending on specific goals of the site, more page views can indicate confusion on the part of the visitor

Possible Impacting Factors

  • Navigational Elements: If it is difficult for visitors to navigate your site, they will often be forced to view more pages as they hunt. Conversely, if your site is difficult to navigate, visitors may leave your site prematurely
  • Content / Range Gaps
Percent Revenue from New Visitors

Important indicator describing “when” you’re most likely to get the visitor to convert.

Total Revenue from New Visitors / Total Revenue = Percent Revenue from New Visitors

Expectation:

Most online business observe that the greatest percentage of revenue comes from returning visitors. Whether the same is true of returning customers depends on whether you’re likely to sell another productto someone once they’ve made the initial purchase, a function of your productassortment and the quality of job you do delivering the first product.

Possible Impacting Factors

  • A sudden increase in this KPI means that the decrease in the likelihood that previous visitors and customers placing orders
  • Customer dis-satisfaction

Shopping Cart Completion Rate

Helps you to isolate problems with the shopping cart functionality.

Total Orders / Total Visits where a Shopping Cart is Started = Cart Completion Rate

Expectation:

If your cart completion rate is low but your cart start rate is high, the most likely problem is visitors experiencing problems with your checkout process.

Possible Impacting Factors

  • Visibility of the buttons or links used to move shoppers through the checkout process
